Industry Partnerships

My industry and academic partnerships reflect a strong commitment to bridging applied research, financial innovation, and education across global contexts.

My Canadian partnerships demonstrate a strong focus on the practical integration of fintech innovation, data science, and regulatory insight. With York Region, I have collaborated on multiple knowledge-sharing initiatives, delivering lectures on AI-driven fraud and its impact on private citizens (Jan 2024) and the post-boom cryptocurrency landscape (Apr 2025), providing thought leadership on how emerging technologies reshape financial ecosystems.

Through Scotiabank’s iLead programme, I delivered industry workshops on Big Data and Analytics (Mar 2019), empowering financial professionals to apply data-driven strategies in decision-making. In the fintech sector, I have led projects such as the Regulatory Analysis for a Toronto-based financial literacy application and the development of heuristic algorithms for blockchain currency pairs, both of which exemplify my commitment to advancing compliance, innovation, and market transparency within Canada’s digital finance landscape.

My international collaborations reflect a dedication to strengthening global fintech education and cross-border knowledge exchange. As Visiting Faculty at Beijing Vocational College of Agriculture in China, I lectured on Financial Information Systems, fostering awareness of digital transformation in finance.

With the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) in Mumbai, I delivered training on Blockchain in DevOps: Implementing Transparent Continuous Delivery, supporting faculty development in emerging technologies.

Additionally, at the National Institute of Financial Management (NIFM) in New Delhi, I served as Guest Lecturer on Fintech subject areas, guiding policymakers and finance professionals through the evolution of technology in financial services. Together, these international engagements highlight my efforts to build an integrated, globally informed perspective on financial innovation and education.

About

I am a passionate academic with a keen interest in fintech research and teaching, having been actively involved for over a decade. My teaching history includes courses on Fintech, Finance, DLT, AI, Cybersecurity, Computational Mathematics, Discrete Mathematics, Ethical Hacking, Database Systems, and Machine Connectivity.

I have presented my research work at several international conferences and have published numerous papers in leading academic journals. My research interests include AI, DeFi, DLT, Regtech, and Fiscal Policy.
